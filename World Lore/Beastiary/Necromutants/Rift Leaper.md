
## LVL 1 Autopsy:
The specimen originates from the native Lupus Caelumbra population of Luna Tenebris, a nocturnal predator adapted to rocky woodland and low-light environments. Baseline anatomy resembles a lean hunting canine, though elongated limbs and unusually dense vestibular structures suggest a species evolved for rapid movement and vertical traversal. Experimental alteration has preserved most of the original skeletal frame, but foreign crystalline deposits are now embedded throughout the nervous system and spinal column.

## LVL 2 Autopsy:
Microscopic analysis confirms the presence of multiple micro-shards embedded within neural tissue and major ganglia. These crystalline inclusions differ from the passive resonance observed in Undead Cadavers or Mutants; they remain highly active, producing fluctuating energy signatures and localized distortions in surrounding matter.

The shards appear deliberately implanted.

Recovered notes reference an experimental procedure involving Harridan and the Magos Biologis’ genetor cadre, wherein Lupus Caelumbra specimens were exposed to carefully fragmented portions of the primary artifact. The intended result was controlled spatial displacement.

The result was… problematic.

## LVL 3 Autopsy:
The organism possesses the unprecedented ability to relocate across short distances without traversing intervening space. Residual readings indicate that this “teleportation” is neither warp translation nor conventional matter transmission. Instead, the specimen appears to momentarily destabilize its physical position before reasserting itself elsewhere.

Field observations reveal severe instability:
- Displacement range fluctuates unpredictably
- Re-entry causes localized tissue stress and hemorrhaging
- Misalignment injuries have been documented in failed jumps

Despite this, the creature demonstrates instinctive use of the ability during ambush and escape behavior.

## LVL 4 Autopsy:
Recovered Tenebris archives identify the Rift Leaper as part of Project Veilstep, among Harridan’s most ambitious collaborations with the Magos Biologis. The objective was not military hunting alone, but technological imitation.

Teleportariums remained vast, resource-intensive systems dependent on calibrated homing beacons and extensive infrastructure. Harridan theorized that if the Shard could grant controlled short-range displacement to living tissue, the principle might one day be replicated for Imperial deployment—portable teleportation without beacon dependence.

The Lupus Caelumbra served as proof of concept.

Project records describe early optimism before repeated containment failures and escalating biological degradation among test subjects. The displacement ability proved real—but impossible to stabilize or scale safely.
