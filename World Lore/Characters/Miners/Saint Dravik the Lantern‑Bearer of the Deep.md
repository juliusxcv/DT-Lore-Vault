### **Honorifics**
- _The Lantern-Bearer_
- _Shield of the Forgotten_
- _Saint of the Deep Veins_
- _The Quiet Flame_

## **Origin Story**

Saint Lumen Dravik was once **Chief Ventmaster Dravik**, a low-ranking but respected worker on the tiny mining moon **Bellum Eternum Secundus‑Theta**, a place so insignificant it barely appeared on Administratum charts.

Dravik was known for:
- keeping ancient ventilation systems running through sheer stubbornness
- personally inspecting the deepest shafts
- refusing to abandon any worker, no matter how dangerous the collapse

He carried an old, battered lumen-lantern — a family relic — said to have belonged to his great-grandmother, a pilgrim who died on the moon generations earlier.

## **The Martyrdom Event**

In 942.M41, a **deep‑shaft seismic event** ruptured a cavern wall, releasing a swarm of unknown xenoforms — pale, eyeless things adapted to the moon’s hollow interior.

The moon had no PDF presence. No Arbites. No support. Just miners.

Dravik:
- rallied the workers
- sealed off the upper shafts
- descended alone into the breach with his lantern and a single industrial cutter

His final vox transmission is still played during sermons:
> “If the Emperor needs a light in the dark, let it be mine.”

He manually detonated the stabilizer charges, collapsing the entire lower cavern system and stopping the xeno incursion at the cost of his life.

The explosion was visible from orbit — a brief, golden flare.

## **Posthumous Recognition**

The Ecclesiarchy initially ignored the event. But the miners didn’t.

They built a shrine around his recovered lantern — found intact, its glow still faintly warm despite the blast. Pilgrims from the moon’s scattered settlements began to visit. Stories spread.

Eventually, the Ministorum dispatched a low-ranking Confessor to “regularize” the cult. He returned with a formal declaration:

**Saint Lumen Dravik, Martyr of the Deep Veins**, recognized as a _Minor Local Saint_.

## **Iconography**

In the chapel on your mining settlement, you’d see:

- A **statue of a broad-shouldered miner** in heavy work gear
- One hand holding a **lumen-lantern**, carved to glow with electrocandles
- The other resting on a **broken cutter**, symbolizing sacrifice
- A halo shaped like a **ventilation fan**
    
- Offerings of:
    - cracked helmets
    - glow-sticks
    - small chunks of ore
    - hand-written pleas for safe shifts

## **Miracles Attributed to Him**

Miners claim:
- his lantern glows brighter before cave-ins
- lost workers hear footsteps and see a warm light guiding them
- xeno vermin avoid shrines dedicated to him
- air quality improves after prayers (officially “coincidence”)

## **Liturgical Role**

He is invoked for:
- safe descent into shafts
- protection from darkness
- endurance under pressure
- unity among workers
- courage in isolation

His feast day is **the Day of the Quiet Flame**, marked by a procession of lanterns through the settlement.
