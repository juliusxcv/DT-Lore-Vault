### From the Journal of Ilia Valev

_(Addendum, separate pages tucked into the back of the ledger)_

---

This is not my entry.

These are Mila’s words, spoken to me this morning while I was recalibrating the air recycler. I am writing them down because she insisted I remember them “the right way.” I will explain them afterward, properly.

She said:

> “I saw the quiet person again.  
> Not a person-person. A tall quiet.  
> It was standing where the trees bend, where the light gets stuck.”

I asked her where she had been.

> “By the rocks past the water pipe. I wasn’t lost. I stayed where you said.”

She told me it didn’t walk.

> “It was already there.  
> And then it was over there instead.”

I corrected her and said shadows move when clouds pass.

She shook her head.

> “No. This one waited.  
> It didn’t like when I looked at it with my eyes.  
> It liked when I looked at it with my ears.”

I asked her what that meant.

> “It was humming the same way the ground does when you’re fixing machines.  
> Not loud. Inside.”

She said she wasn’t scared.

This concerned me more than if she had been.

> “It didn’t want to come closer.  
> It just wanted to know if I was loud.”

That is when I stopped her.

Children anthropomorphize. They assign intention where there is none. The forest here is strange, yes, but not alive in the way stories suggest. I told her this.

She accepted it too quickly.

Before leaving, she added one more thing, almost as an afterthought:

> “It was thinner than you.  
> And taller than the Angel.”

I asked her what Angel.

She frowned, like I had missed something obvious.

> “The green one. He’s loud. This one is not.”

I told her firmly that there was no such thing, that tired minds make connections they shouldn’t, that the light here plays tricks.

She nodded.

Children always do, when they sense a line has been drawn.

Still, I walked the perimeter myself later. I found nothing. No tracks. No damage. No readings outside acceptable variance.

And yet the trees were quiet in a way I have only felt once before.

I have decided not to correct her further.

If this is imagination, it will fade.

And if it is not—

Then it is better she believes it is quiet, distant, and uninterested in us.

For now.
