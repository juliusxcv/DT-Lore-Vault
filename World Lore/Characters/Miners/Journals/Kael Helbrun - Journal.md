### Perimeter Walk
Wind readings steady today. Tree line unchanged. I took a longer route back than planned because the shorter one felt monotonous.

That sounds strange written out. I mean that it felt like I had already walked it, even though I hadn’t. Probably just fatigue.

Boot seals holding. One tear repaired with patch compound. I should replace the boots entirely next cycle, but supplies are tight and these will last.

---
### Two anomalies today. 
Minor. Both dismissible.

One sensor spike that didn’t repeat. One distance misjudgment on a familiar route. I corrected for both and moved on.

Later, I found my own footprints overlapping in a way that suggested I had turned around earlier than I remembered.

Memory drift, likely. The light here does odd things to depth perception.

Still, I marked the spot.

I don’t like that I felt watched without seeing anything.

---
### I do not trust my instruments anymore.
That sentence would have horrified me a month ago.

The readings are consistent, but consistency means nothing when the environment itself is behaving inconsistently. Light refraction is within expected variance and yet… I cannot judge distance reliably anymore. Trees feel closer than they are. Paths feel longer.

On my last survey run, I turned around because I felt something behind me.

There was nothing.
I stood there for several minutes anyway, unable to move.
I do not like admitting that.

---
### The light behaved today.
I do not have better language for it than that.

Refraction normal. Distances consistent. The forest still feels watchful, but no longer… intent.

The stranger walked part of the route with me. I did not ask him to. He simply did.

When the wind shifted suddenly, he stopped and listened. Then nodded, as if satisfied, and kept walking.

Whatever he listens for, it was quiet today.


---
### Company
I took the long route today. The stranger walked part of it with me.

He doesn’t like open sky much. Keeps glancing upward, then back to the ground. I asked if the light bothered him.

He said, “I prefer knowing where threats can come from.”

That seems excessive for a mining settlement, but I suppose everyone brings their own habits.

We walked in silence for a while. The wind shifted, and he stopped abruptly. Listened. Told me to wait.

Nothing happened.

When I asked what he heard, he said, “Nothing. That’s what matters.”

---
### Clear Skies
The forest was quieter today.

Not empty. Just… settled.

Wind patterns normalized. I took readings twice to be sure. No anomalies worth recording. I wrote that down and then stared at it for a long time, wondering when “nothing to report” had started to feel like a gift.

I walked the ridge alone.

For the first time in months, I did not feel the urge to look over my shoulder.

That should be relief.

It felt like goodbye.
