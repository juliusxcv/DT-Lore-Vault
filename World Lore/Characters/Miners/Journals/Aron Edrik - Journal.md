### Loose Page, Found Between Entries
We have been here a long time now.

Long enough that this place stopped feeling temporary. Long enough that the idea of leaving feels like failure instead of prudence.

That makes me nervous.

Good runs make people careless. Not because they forget danger—but because they forget _change_.

If something has shifted, it did so quietly. Not with alarms or warnings. Just with absence.

One less voice at supper.  
One less shadow on the lift platform.

Tomorrow, we will keep working. That is what sensible people do.

But tonight, I find myself wondering whether the moon has finally noticed us back.

### The lights are behaving strangely.
Not flickering. Not failing. Just… slow. As if they hesitate before deciding how bright to be.

I’ve checked the load three times. Everything is fine.

People keep asking me if it’s safe.

I tell them yes.

I wish I believed myself.

Someone scratched over my chalk marks again. This time with their fingernails, I think. The lines are uneven.

I erased them.

### Breaker logs normal.
Lights steady.

No unexplained erasures on the walls.

The stranger pointed out a loose coupling I’d missed. Didn’t criticize. Just indicated it with two fingers and waited until I saw it myself.

When I thanked him, he nodded like thanks were unnecessary.

Maybe they are.

No one is calling this hope.
That would be tempting fate.

But the disappearances have stopped.  
The nights are quieter.  
The shadows feel like shadows again.

We don’t know who he is. We don’t know how long he’ll stay.

But when he walks through the settlement, people straighten without realizing it. When he stands nearby, the light seems less cruel.

If this is coincidence, it is a kind one.

For now, that is enough.

### Unexpected Help
The new guy helped me reroute a stubborn coupling today. The guy is huuge.

I didn’t ask for help. He just showed up, crouched beside the panel, and pointed out a stress fracture I’d missed. Hairline. Easy to overlook.

He knew the system layout better than he should. When I asked how, he said, “Most things fail the same way.”

That’s not an answer, but it’s not wrong either.

I offered him a meal after. He declined, but stood nearby while I ate. Didn’t stare. Didn’t wander.

Like he was keeping watch.

### All systems nominal.
I’ve written that line hundreds of times. Today it felt heavier than usual.

Breaker 3 didn’t trip. The corridor lights held steady. The [[Chalk marks]] I keep finding erased were untouched today. I don’t know why that bothered me.

I ate alone by the panel room and realized he used to stand there sometimes. Never interfering. Just present.

Like he was making sure the systems behaved.
They did today.

I wish he’d stayed.
