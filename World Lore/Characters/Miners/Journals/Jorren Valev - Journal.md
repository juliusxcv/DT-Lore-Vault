### Maintenance
We adjusted the grinders again today. Efficiency is down another fraction of a percent, but still within acceptable loss. Acceptable keeps shrinking as a word.

The kids helped clean filters after supper. Mila insists she likes the noise because it “means things are working.” I told her she can keep that opinion as long as she doesn’t stick her hands where they don’t belong.

We’re burning through gloves faster than expected. I’ll requisition more.


---
### Throughput down today. 
Not by much. Still within margin.

I keep writing that line. “Within margin.” It’s starting to feel like an excuse instead of a measurement.

We’ve had three missed shifts this quarter. Two accounted for. One not.

I ran the numbers twice and came up with the same answer: statistically insignificant.

Statistics don’t help when you’re missing a person.

Mila asked me why everyone was whispering. I told her they weren’t. She didn’t believe me.

---
### Production is irrelevant now.
I still log it. That feels important. But the numbers don’t mean what they used to. We’re short-handed enough that efficiency discussions feel obscene.

The processors echo differently when fewer people are on shift. I never realized how much sound comes from presence rather than machinery.

Mila hasn’t slept properly in days. She says the shadows in her room move when she closes her eyes. I told her shadows don’t move.

She asked me how I know.

I didn’t answer.

---
### The machines ran clean today.
No harmonics. No irregular vibration. No sudden silences that make you hold your breath. I kept waiting for the moment something would seize or scream.

It didn’t.

The stranger stood by the processors for a while. Didn’t touch anything. Just watched. When he finally left, the noise stayed even.

Mila says she slept through the night.

I don’t care why.

### Log Entry 2
The big one passed through the processors today.

I noticed because the floor vibrated differently when he walked. Not louder. Just… heavier. Like when a fully loaded cart rolls past instead of an empty one.

He helped clear a jam in Grinder C. Didn’t touch the controls, just lifted the housing while I reset it. The machine noise dropped by half once he steadied it. That makes no sense, but I’m not questioning results.

He watches equipment like it might misbehave if he looks away.

Mila asked him why he wears so many layers. He said, “Because I’m used to colder places.”

She accepted that immediately.

---
### Looking Forward
He usually passed through before second shift.

Today the floor felt lighter.

That’s nonsense. I know that. But the vibration pattern was different. Familiar, but thinner. Like a machine after a part is removed—not broken, just… missing something that used to dampen the noise.

Grinder C ran clean all day. No jams. No sudden harmonics. I should be pleased. Well, I am pleased! Still...

Mila asked where the tall man was.

I said he’d moved on to another job.

She nodded and went back to drawing. Later I saw she’d left one corner of the page empty, like she was saving space.

I didn’t tell her to finish it.
