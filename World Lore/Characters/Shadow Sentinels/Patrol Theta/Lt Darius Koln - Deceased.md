### NPC Profile: Lieutenant Darius Koln

**Role:** Commanding Officer, Topside Patrol Theta, Shadow Sentinels **Status:** Deceased (classified)

---

#### Appearance

Koln was lean and angular, with the kind of face that looked permanently tired — deep-set eyes under a heavy brow, a jaw that hadn't been clean-shaven in years. His matte blue carapace always had one pauldron slightly misaligned, a habit his squad found equal parts endearing and infuriating. In his final days, those who noticed said his eyes had taken on a faint luminescence. They didn't mention it to each other until after.

---

#### Personality

A pragmatist by training, a believer by choice. Koln ran a tight patrol but wasn't a hard man — he knew his troopers' names, their home worlds, their fears. He had a particular habit of keeping a personal log, partly as duty, partly because he trusted words more than people. He was the kind of officer who followed orders not out of blind obedience but because he had genuinely reasoned his way to the same conclusion. That made what happened to him worse.

---

#### Agenda _(at time of corruption)_

The resonance didn't twist Koln into a monster. It convinced him. His final entries suggest he became certain that the Lockdown Protocol was the true heresy — that whatever was inside needed to be _released_, not contained. He believed he was saving his squad. He may not have been entirely wrong about the danger. He was entirely wrong about the solution.

---

#### Secret

Koln was the only member of Patrol Theta who had legitimate access to the Theta Gate override — a secondary clearance card issued by Harridan personally for emergency egress scenarios. He never used it. When the squad "dealt" with him, Sergeant Varn stripped the card and destroyed it, then logged it as _lost during the incident_. What Varn didn't find was Koln's personal backup — a ghost-coded copy Koln had quietly made after the first time his head started filling with voices. Old soldier's instinct: always have an exit.

---

#### The Locker

Koln kept a personal footlocker in the Garrison's officers' annex — never locked, because, as he used to say, _"a locked box tells everyone exactly where to look."_ Inside, beneath a folded regimental banner and a cracked data-slate with his personal logs:

- Astartes-Pattern Bolt Pistol
- Sanctus Blade Standard
- His final personal log

---

#### The False Hope: The Access Card

Varn will confirm, if pressed, that Koln _had_ an access card. He'll also confirm, with the flat affect of a man who has rehearsed this, that it was destroyed. He'll even show them the entry in his field log. It's not a lie — Varn destroyed _a_ card. He's simply omitting that he doesn't know about the ghost copy.

The ghost copy is hidden inside the lining of Koln's regimental banner — stitched into the hem with surgical thread, the kind Medic Veylan uses. Whether Veylan helped him hide it, or simply doesn't know it's there, is left for the GM to decide. [NEW CANON — card location]

**What the players discover when they find it:** the card is real, it's active — and it triggers an immediate alert in the Security Command Deck when used. Koln had disabled the silent flag on his copy so he'd know if someone else found it. The system still thinks he's alive.

#### Last Journal Entries of Darius Koln

Three nights without sleep. Maybe four.

It isn't a voice. I need to be precise about this because if someone finds this log and thinks I'm describing possession they'll burn it and stop reading. It isn't a voice. It's more like — standing in a vast empty hall and hearing the echo of something that hasn't been said yet.

It doesn't want anything from me. That's what I can't reconcile with everything I was taught. Everything in the doctrine says the warp _hungers_. This doesn't hunger. It _waits_.

Varn has been watching me. He has a specific look — I trained it into him — the one that comes before a decision he's already made. I know what that look means. I wrote the protocol he'd be following.

I'm not going to run. I'm not going to fight it.

But the Lockdown is wrong. I'm certain of it now. Harridan didn't contain whatever is down there. He compressed it. And pressure, given enough time, always finds a way out.

I've hidden the card. If I'm right, someone will need it. If I'm wrong
—then it won't matter.

_End of entry._
