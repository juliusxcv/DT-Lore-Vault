 ### **Subject Profile**  
- Designation: OGR‑271 (Cerebrum Augmented)  
- Classification: Enhanced Ogryn Infantry – Unauthorized xenotech augmentation via Ork neuro‑implants  
- Status: Active; currently under high‑security containment  
- Current Evaluation: Exhibits above‑average intelligence and tactical acumen compared to standard Ogryn units. However, his unsanctioned cranial modifications result in unpredictable decision‑making and potential ideological instability.

 

 **Findings & Threat Assessment**  
• Unorthodox Augmentation: Post‑battlefield recovery operations discovered that OGR‑271’s cerebrum was fitted with invasive Ork implants. These implants, although not of Imperial design, confer a level of strategic insight and rapid cognitive processing remarkably atypical for his species.  
• Enhanced Tactical Capabilities: Field reports attest to his ability to formulate effective albeit unconventional battle strategies—an asset that, if controlled, could offer significant tactical advantages.  
• Behavioral Instability & Ideological Risk: The xenotech source of the implants introduces erratic neural interference. Subject behavior has shown impulsivity and a predisposition toward unorthodox thought patterns. These fluctuations raise serious concerns regarding potential heretical ideation and the subversion of Imperium‑mandated combat doctrine.

• Operational Concerns: Although his enhanced intellect offers a rare tactical edge, the duality of his human (or rather, Ogryn) instincts and augmented cognition may render him a double‑edged sword. His capacity for both brilliant decision‑making and reckless, unfiltered aggression necessitates immediate further study.


 ## Recommended Course of Action  

1. Forensic Re‑Evaluation: Subject’s neural implants must be analyzed and, if possible, recalibrated by Mechanicus tech‑priests experienced with xenos augmentation.  

2. Extended Containment & Surveillance: OGR‑271 is to remain in a high‑security cell fitted with neural dampening fields and under constant observation for any signs of psychological or ideological deviation.  

3. Conditional Tactical Deployment: Should reconditioning prove successful, subject may be considered for experimental deployment in highly controlled engagements, subject to immediate de‑activation on any breach of protocols.  

4. Immediate Neutralization: In the event that his implants prove irredeemably unstable or lead to further heretical behaviors, prompt termination is advised to prevent wider contamination of Imperial forces.
