#### ACT 1 — Finishing the First Squad

**Situation:** 4–6 necromutants remain from the original wave, scattered across the map. Some are behind cover. One may be dragging itself toward the tower with a broken leg, relentless and silent. They are not coordinating anymore — their command signal is degraded — but they don't stop.

**GM-side objective:** Let the players feel dominant. They've earned it. Give them clean kills, good angles, satisfying shots. This is deliberate — you're building the false security you'll pull out from under them.

**Player-facing objective:** Eliminate all remaining hostiles.

**Atmospheric detail:** The moon's surface is dead silent except for the crack of gunfire and the wet sound of things that should be still refusing to lie down. One necromutant, shot through the chest, keeps walking for two full activations before its legs finally agree to stop. Nobody jokes about it.

**Pacing note:** End this act cleanly. Don't drag it. The moment the last necromutant drops, hard cut to the lull.

---

#### THE LULL — False Security

**Duration:** One short in-game beat. Long enough to matter.

The vox is still dead. The tower is intact. Someone finds a Sentinel ration pack in the corner — sealed, Imperial issue, recent. Nobody comments on what that implies.

Give the players time to loot. Necromutants carry degraded equipment — corroded lasguns, cracked carapace fragments, nothing valuable — but one body yields something worth noting:

> A security keycard, half-melted, bearing the Mechanicus cog alongside a facility designation stamp. Sub-Level Theta. It no longer functions. But it existed, which means functional ones did too.

Let them breathe. Let them think they've won.

Then — at the worst possible moment, mid-sentence if someone is talking — the ground trembles. Low. Rhythmic. Getting closer.

#### ACT 2, BEAT 1 — Aberrants and the Ogryn

**Situation:** From the eastern approach, the first new shapes emerge from the dark. Wrong proportions. Too fast for their size.

**Moonspawned Aberrants** arrive in a loose pack — 4 to 6, depending on table size. They move like animals but don't behave like them. Each one is visibly different from the last, the resonance rewriting them in real time. One has too many joints in its forelimbs. Another's jaw has split into something that shouldn't work but does. At the start of each activation, roll a mutation — a new limb, enhanced speed, hardened plating, a shriek that forces a nerve check. They're not the threat. They're the distraction.

**The Necromutant Ogryn** arrives on turn 2 of this beat, cresting the ridge alone. It doesn't rush. It doesn't need to. Whatever it was before the resonance took hold — a security enforcer, a labor servitor, something larger — it is now a walking demolition event. Massive HP pool. Charges the tower directly. Its objective isn't the players. It's the _structure_. It wants the tower down.

**GM-side objective:** Force the players out of their fortified position or make holding it costly. The Ogryn targets the tower walls specifically.

**Player-facing objective:** Stop the Ogryn before it brings the tower down. Deal with the aberrants without letting them tie up everyone.

**Complication:** Aberrant mutations stack. An aberrant that survives two turns becomes genuinely dangerous. Players who ignore them to focus the Ogryn will regret it.

**Atmospheric detail:** The Ogryn makes no sound. No roar, no impact grunt. It just _moves_, and the ground registers it before anything else does.

---

#### ACT 2, BEAT 2 — Rift Leapers and the Mindspike Horror

**Timing:** Deploy these when the Ogryn is at half HP or the players look like they're stabilizing. The moment they think they have a handle on it.

**Rift Leapers** — 2 to 3 — blink into existence at the edges of the map simultaneously. Cloaked beyond 6 inches, teleporting 12 inches per activation, never staying where they were targeted. They don't engage directly at first — they circle, they flank, they appear briefly and vanish. They're testing the perimeter. Their purpose is psychological as much as tactical: nowhere feels safe anymore.

**The Mindspike Horror** emerges last, from beneath the surface — a fissure cracks open near the tower's base and it pulls itself up, wrong and skittering, a cluster of human faces on arachnid limbs, all of them moving independently. Its ranged attack doesn't target bodies — it targets minds. Any psyker in the warband immediately becomes a liability: the Horror's presence causes psychic interference, forcing warp-related abilities to roll on a hazard table rather than function normally.

**GM-side objective:** Create genuine crisis. The players should feel the map has become hostile in every direction.

**Player-facing objective:** Survive. There's no clean solution here — every choice costs something.

**Complication:** The Mindspike Horror prioritizes psykers. If no psyker is present, it fixates on whoever used a special ability last. It remembers.

**Atmospheric detail:** The Horror's faces are recognizable as human. Some players may notice they're all the same face — a Mechanicus adept, repeated, each one frozen in a different expression of the same final moment.

#### ACT 3 — The Sentinels Drop In

**Timing:** Peak of Beat 2. Rift leapers active, Horror on the table, Ogryn still standing or freshly down. The worst possible moment.

From the northern perimeter — the direction nobody has been watching — eight figures in matte blue carapace emerge from the dark at a dead run, refractor fields flickering. They are disciplined, fast, and they immediately take up fire positions around the tower.

They do not announce themselves. The first thing they do is shoot.

**Sentinel deployment:**

- Varn and Drask take the northern approach, cover arcs on the tower entrance
- Ryn sets up elevated on a ridge — he has a bead on every player operative immediately
- Bronn Kade sets up the heavy weapon on the western flank
- Remaining troopers push the aberrants and rift leapers on sight — they know these enemies, they've seen them before

**Initial posture:** Hostile to players. Not frenzied — _disciplined_ hostile. Varn issues one challenge, once, loudly:

> "Identify yourselves and drop your weapons or we will put you down. You have five seconds."

He means it. He is also simultaneously engaging a rift leaper with his sidearm. He's done this before.

**The negotiation under fire:**

This is the session's centrepiece — players must convince Varn they're Inquisition while actively fighting. Possible approaches:

- **Inquisitorial seals or rosettes** — Varn recognizes them. Doesn't fully trust them. Stands down to _conditional_ non-hostile, still watching.
- **Invoking Harridan's name** — gets Varn's full attention immediately. His posture changes. He doesn't lower his weapon but he stops the five-second clock.
- **Tactical cooperation** — a player covers a Sentinel's exposed flank without being asked. Drask notices. She says one word to Varn. He reassesses.
- **Veylan** — if she's present in this deployment, she's the softest entry point. She's already watching the players differently than Varn is.

**Scaling note:** Once Sentinels shift to conditional allied, they focus the Horror and ogryn. Rift leapers remain contested. The table should feel like it tips — not to safe, but to _survivable_.

---

#### Session End Condition

The Mindspike Horror goes down or retreats into the fissure. Rift leapers disengage. The Ogryn is down or has been for a while.

Varn and the players are standing in the same space, weapons lowered but not holstered.

He looks at the carnage. Looks at them.

> "You fight like Inquisition. Doesn't mean I trust you."

_Beat._

> "Get inside. We talk."
