### I. **Initial Survey and Designation**

- Luna Tenebris was formally identified as BT3-MOON-02 during orbital mapping operations conducted by the Adeptus Mechanicus Explorator Corps in M38.991.
- Preliminary scans indicated moderate mineral presence, stable atmospheric composition, and low native biosphere hostility.
- The moon was designated as a **Category IV Extraction Site** under the Helios Sub-Cluster Resource Allocation Directive.

### II. **Colonization Timeline**

- **M38.992:** Deployment of initial survey teams and automated mining units.
- **M38.993–M38.995:** Construction of primary infrastructure, including Research Station Tenebris, Theta-Gate Landing Platform, and auxiliary habitation domes.
- **M38.996:** Arrival of penal labor contingents and low-clearance civilian technicians.
- **M38.997–M39.002:** Expansion of subterranean mining shafts and installation of atmospheric stabilizers.
- **M39.003:** Commencement of full-scale mineral extraction operations.
- **M39.014:** Peak operational capacity reached; population estimated at 3,200 personnel.

### III. **Operational Challenges**

- **Environmental Conditions:**
    - Persistent low-light atmospheric state due to orbital alignment with Bellum Eternum Tertius.
    - Psychological degradation reported among long-term personnel.
    - High incidence of visual disorientation and circadian disruption.
    
- **Infrastructure Limitations:**
    - Repeated structural failures in deep-shaft mining zones.
    - Inadequate yield-to-effort ratio compared to alternate sites within the system.
    - Vox relay interference due to planetary magnetic anomalies.
    

### IV. **Decommissioning and Withdrawal**

- **M39.015–M40.221:** Luna Tenebris repurposed as a secondary logistics node during the Bellum Eternum Tertius conflict.
- **M40.222:** Resource extraction operations formally terminated.
- **M40.223–M40.229:** Gradual withdrawal of personnel and equipment.
- **M40.230:** Site reclassified as Dormant Zone; all remaining assets placed under passive Inquisitorial observation.

### V. **Post-Colonial Status**

- **Population:** Uninhabited (officially)
- **Infrastructure:** Derelict; partial integrity in select facilities
- **Strategic Value:** Negligible
- **Environmental Stability:** Moderate
- **Inquisitorial Interest:** Elevated due to anomalous readings

**The Abandonment of Luna Tenebris**
Excerpt from Inquisitorial Archive Entry IX-CT/44.2 – Restricted Access

Though once surveyed as a promising mining colony during the early expansion of the Bellum Eternum system, Luna Tenebris was never fully embraced by its settlers. The moon’s mineral yields were modest, and its cave systems—while extensive—proved unstable and difficult to navigate. But it was not the geology that drove the exodus.

It was the light.

Orbiting in perpetual twilight beneath the shadow of Bellum Eternum Tertius, Luna Tenebris never experiences true day or night. Its skies remain locked in a soft, pastel glow—lavender, rose, and pale gold—colors that should evoke peace, but instead stir unease. Shadows stretch unnaturally long. They move when they shouldn’t. The human eye, evolved for contrast and clarity, finds no rest here. Depth perception falters. Time loses meaning.

Miners reported strange phenomena:  
- Tools misplaced and found in impossible locations.  
- Echoes that didn’t match their source.  
- Lights seen in the caves, moving with purpose, yet no life detected.  
- Dreams of voices, speaking in languages no one knew but everyone understood.  

No deaths. No attacks. Just a slow erosion of certainty. A creeping doubt. A collective decision to leave.

Within two decades, the colony was abandoned. The remaining infrastructure—habitation domes, mining rigs, and vox towers—was left to decay. The local fauna reclaimed the land. The flora grew thicker, stranger. The caves, once mapped, became unknowable again.
