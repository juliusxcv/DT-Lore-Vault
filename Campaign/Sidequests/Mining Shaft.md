## Modulators
### 6 DORMANT THREAT FIELD
**Trigger:** First action taken in the room.

**Hint**: 

**Abstract Rule:**  
The room begins with **0 Threat**.  
Each **Shoot, Charge, Dash, or Fall Back** adds +1 Threat.  
Each **Interact, Drop, or Wait (pass activation)** removes −1 Threat.

**Threat 1** → Nothing
**Threat 2** → Nothing
**Threat 3** → enemies spawn.
**Threat 4** → enemies spawn.
**Threat 5** → enemies spawn.
**Threat 6** → enemies spawn.

**Non-Combat Solutions:**
- Slow advance
- Heavy use of Interact
- Item drops to manipulate space

**Failure Escalation:**  
Enemies spawn **stronger** the higher the Threat when triggered.


### 4 COGNITIVE INTERFERENCE
**Trigger:** Upon entry.

**Hint**: Think before you act, don't be a copy cat.

**Abstract Rule:**  
Operatives cannot repeat the **same action** as the previous operative.

**Non-Combat Solutions:**
- Rotate Interacts, Moves, Pick Ups
- Deliberately waste low-impact actions to free others

**Failure Escalation:**  
Breaking the rule causes the operative to gain **Weakened** and triggers a hostile manifestation next round.

### 17 RESONANCE CASCADE
**Trigger:** Upon entering the room a single neutral marker is placed anywhere in the room.

**Abstract Rule:**  
The marker must be **moved to the exit**.
The Marker has Unstable 1 and Heavy(Dash only)
Can't use Weapons while carrying the marker.
Dropping the marker adds 1 to it's Unstable Rule.

**Player Agency:**  
Decide who carries, who escorts, who sacrifices AP.

**Non-Combat Solutions:**
- Cooperative carrying
- Dropping and leapfrogging
- Clearing a safe path first

**Failure Escalation:**  
Dropping it too often corrupts it → Spawn Enemies according to it's Unstable Value

**Unstable**
1 - 1 Enemy Unit
2 - 2 Enemy Units
3 - 3 Enemy Units
4 - 4 Enemy Units
5 - 5 Enemy Units
6 - 6 Enemy Units

### 15 LIGHTS OUT
**Trigger:** Immediately upon room entry.

**Hint:** The room is plunged into **absolute darkness**.

**Abstract Rule:**  
- **Visibility is reduced to 2”**.
- Ranged attacks **cannot be made** unless the weapon has **Silent** or the target is within 2”.
- Movement beyond 3” in a single action requires a **risk roll**.

Whenever an operative:
- Dashes
- Charges

Roll a D6:
- **1–2:** Collision → end your turn immediately
- **3–4:** Disoriented → movement ends early
- **5–6:** Safe movement

### 3 NOT YET
**Trigger:** First action taken in the room.

**Hint**: Everything goes wrong—quietly.
Sound dulls. Motion lags by a fraction of a second.  
Reality feels _out of sync_, like a cog skipping a tooth.

**Set up:**  
At its center stands **Lord Inquisitor Seraphina Valeria**—  
older.

Not weakened.  
Sharper. Colder. Scarred in ways the party has not yet earned.

Opposite her: an **Eldar Farseer**, robed in pale wraithbone and void-black runes.  
His presence bends the air. His shadow falls _before_ he moves.

They are mid-conversation.

Not arguing.  
Not threatening.
**Discussing something necessary.**

Fragments only—never the full truth:
- “—the shard cannot be destroyed. Only—”
- “—you _know_ what it will cost your species.”
- “Better one wound than an extinction.”

Then—
The party materializes.

#### THE REACTION (IMPORTANT)

Both figures react **instantly**.

This is not a dramatic pause.  
This is professionals realizing a catastrophic anomaly.

- The Farseer’s head snaps toward the party, eyes widening in genuine alarm.
- Seraphina’s hand is already halfway to her weapon before she stops herself.

For the first—and only—time the players will ever see it:

> **Seraphina Valeria is shaken.**

She stares directly at the party.
Not confused.
Not angry.
**Horrified.**

> “No.  
> No—this is wrong.”

The Farseer speaks next, voice layered, echoing with futures collapsing:

> “They are _early_.  
> By decades.”

Seraphina turns on him, furious and afraid in equal measure:
> “You said the strands were isolated.”
> “They were,” the Farseer replies. “Until _you_ survived.”

---

#### THE INTERRUPTION

Seraphina steps forward.

She does **not** address the group as assets.  
Not as operatives.  
Not as expendable tools.
She addresses them as something else.

> “You are not supposed to be here.  
> Not yet.  
> And if you remember this, everything breaks.”

She locks eyes with each of them in turn.

> “I’m sorry.”

The Farseer raises his staff. Runes ignite, then **fracture**.

> “Go back,” he says sharply.  
> “Forget this moment—or die before you matter.”

---

#### THE FORCED RETURN

There is no attack.
No save.
No resistance.

Reality folds **inward**, not outward.

The last thing the party hears—quiet, almost drowned by time tearing itself apart—is Seraphina’s voice, no longer cold, no longer commanding:

> “Please…  
> not like this.”

---

#### AFTERMATH (BACK IN THE PRESENT)

The party reappears exactly where they should be.
No time has passed.
No damage taken.
No conditions applied.

But—
- All flames burn normally again.
- All sounds return slightly _too loud_.
- One random player notices their hand shaking.

If asked, Seraphina Valeria **will never acknowledge this event**.

Not now. Not later. Not ever.

The Eldar Farseer is never mentioned again.

---

#### GM NOTES (DO NOT READ ALOUD)

- This confirms:
    - Seraphina survives far into the future
    - She cooperates with xenos under extreme circumstances
    - The party is _instrumental_, not incidental
- The Farseer’s alarm implies:
    - The timeline is fragile
    - The party’s survival is not guaranteed
- The phrase **“until you survived”** should haunt them

This scene should leave players with one takeaway:
> **They are already part of something larger than they understand.**
