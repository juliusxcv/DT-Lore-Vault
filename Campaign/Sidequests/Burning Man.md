**Resolving the Mystery:**
The Burning man can be relieved of his dark fate by pulling out the Drukhari Dagger.



#### **Dialogue with the Burning Corpse**

**Initial Interaction: The First Screams**

(_The flames crackle louder as the corpse twitches, its voice raw and guttural._) 
> **Burning Corpse:** "No... no! Dont leave me! Dont leave me to burn! It’s everywhere! I can feel it crawling in me—please, _end it_!"
 
(_It screams again, shuddering against the stake._)

(_If the players persist in questioning it..._) 
> **Burning Corpse:** "_Who are you_? Who sent you? No—no, you cannot be here! They’ll kill you all...

**Revealing Its Identity: After Several Tries**

(_After further prodding, the corpse speaks in fragmented sentences, barely coherent amidst its agony._) 
> **Burning Corpse:** "I... I was _[[Drakos Kael]],_ Imperial scientist, lower levels. We... we studied them—the artifact! We should have destroyed it. But they came for us! The shadows with knives... they cursed me, _made me this_! Hah, but now the dagger... the dagger keeps me alive. Keeps me burning!"

(_The corpse sobs, its voice cracking with despair._) 

> **Burning Corpse:** "Take it—take it out! And let me die! The dagger—those xenos filth—so cruel, so cruel! They laughed as they did it... I hear them still!"

**When the Dagger Is Removed**
(_The players remove the Drukhari dagger lodged in the corpse’s spine, an ornate weapon shimmering with eldritch energy. The moment it is pulled free, the flames vanish, and the corpse collapses into lifeless ash. Its final words are quiet, barely audible._) 

> **Burning Corpse (dying):** "Thank you... thank you. Beware the artifact... it is... _alive._ You must... stop it... before it claims you too..."

**Role of the Drukhari Curse**
The curse placed on Drakos Kael reflects the sadistic ingenuity of the Drukhari, who relish in suffering that lingers long past death. The dagger not only cursed him to burn endlessly but tethered his soul to torment, forcing him to endure and speak in a way designed to terrify anyone who finds him. Removing the dagger severs the connection, finally freeing him.

**Reward or Consequences**
- **The Drukhari Dagger:** The players can keep the weapon, though its cursed nature may bring unforeseen dangers. It could be a key to understanding how the artifact operates or serve as bait for future Drukhari encounters.
