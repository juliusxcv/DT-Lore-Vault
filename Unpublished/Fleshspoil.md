
## LVL 1 Autopsy:
The designated _Fleshspoil_ possesses no stable anatomy and cannot be traced to a single host organism. Instead, the specimen consists of multiple human bodies fused into a singular biological mass. Limbs emerge without symmetry, skeletal structures overlap and interlock, and several partially integrated torsos remain visibly distinct within the greater organism. Internal motion persists even in severed sections, suggesting localized biological autonomy across the mass.

Most specimens display exposed musculature and incomplete fusion patterns, as though the process of incorporation remains ongoing.

## LVL 2 Autopsy:
Unlike other Tenebris mutants, the Fleshspoil exhibits no dependence on deliberate augmentation or implanted structures. Growth occurs through direct physical incorporation of biological material.

Observed feeding behavior follows a consistent pattern:

- Immobilization of prey
- Partial consumption
- Extended physical contact between corpse and primary mass
- Progressive tissue fusion over several hours

Newly acquired flesh does not digest conventionally. Instead, it is absorbed into the organism, triggering extensive mutation and structural rearrangement. Limbs may reposition, organs duplicate, and muscle density increases unpredictably following each assimilation event.

No upper limit to growth has been observed.

## LVL 3 Autopsy:
Cellular analysis reveals permanent instability driven by continuous Shard resonance exposure. Fleshspoil tissue remains locked in an uninterrupted adaptive cycle:

- Necrosis
- Regeneration
- Mutation
- Reorganization

These processes occur simultaneously and never appear to reach equilibrium. Internal scans reveal partially functional organs from multiple donors, some still genetically identifiable as separate individuals despite complete fusion.

Unlike the Moonspawned Aberrant, whose hunger drives mutation, the Fleshspoil mutates simply because it exists.

## LVL 4 Autopsy:
Recovered Tenebris records confirm a crucial distinction:

The Fleshspoil was **not** the product of Harridan or the Magos Biologis’ experimentation.

It emerged spontaneously following the collapse of Facility Tenebris.

Personnel stationed closest to the Mutation Chambers perished during the containment failure. In the months that followed, their corpses—saturated by prolonged exposure to the Shard’s resonance—began to exhibit unprecedented post-mortem activity. Witness reports describe dead bodies swelling, softening, and gradually merging where they lay. Floors became carpeted with living tissue. Entire morgues fused into singular masses before rupturing outward into mobile forms.

The Fleshspoil represents the natural consequence of unrestricted resonance exposure upon concentrated death.
