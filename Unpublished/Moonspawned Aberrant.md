
## LVL 1 Autopsy:
The specimen displays clear anatomical origins traceable to the native Canis Murus population of Luna Tenebris. However, baseline physiology has been catastrophically altered. Skeletal growth exceeds natural proportions by nearly three hundred percent, with grotesque muscular hypertrophy surrounding the shoulders and thorax. Most striking is the cranial structure: the jaws have split longitudinally into two independent mandibles, exposing multiple layers of replacement dentition adapted for tearing rather than biting. The abdominal cavity remains partially open, pierced outward by an overgrown ribcage as though internal expansion exceeded the limits of natural anatomy.

The creature appears perpetually starved, its own body stretched beyond design in pursuit of feeding.

## LVL 2 Autopsy:
Internal examination reveals profound genetic instability unlike that observed in Undead Cadavers or Mutants. Tissue analysis suggests the Aberrant incorporates biological material from consumed organisms directly into its own morphology. Foreign bone structures, incompatible muscle fibers, and partially integrated organs are distributed throughout the body with little regard for natural arrangement. Several samples contained human osteological fragments fused seamlessly into native tissue.

This phenomenon appears cumulative.

The more the organism feeds, the more unstable and physically expansive it becomes.

It does not digest in the conventional sense.
It assimilates.

## LVL 3 Autopsy:
Despite extreme mutation, the specimen retains remnants of predatory Canis Murus behavior. It stalks rather than charges, isolates vulnerable prey, and displays territorial instincts centered around feeding grounds and corpse deposits. Yet these instincts are overshadowed by a pathological feeding drive that never appears satisfied.

Dissection of the thoracic cavity revealed severe organ displacement caused by continuous skeletal growth. The ribcage itself punctures digestive tissue and surrounding musculature, injuries that should prove fatal but instead remain locked in a state of incomplete healing. Pain responses were either absent or ignored entirely.

## LVL 4 Autopsy:
Recovered laboratory fragments indicate the Moonspawned Aberrant was not a spontaneous mutation, but among the earliest confirmed experiments conducted jointly by Magos Biologis and Lord Inquisitor Harridan. Their objective appears explicit: the creation of a perfect pursuit organism capable of surviving Luna Tenebris’ hostile terrain while tracking and eliminating prey indefinitely. Native Canis Murus specimens were selected for their pack instincts, endurance, and adaptability before exposure to controlled Shard resonance and experimental mutagenic procedures.

The results exceeded intended parameters.

The creature gained extraordinary regenerative capacity and predatory efficiency—but proved impossible to stabilize. Feeding behavior intensified exponentially, while absorbed biomass accelerated further mutation beyond containment predictions. Official notes terminate abruptly after repeated references to “adaptive success exceeding handler authority.”
