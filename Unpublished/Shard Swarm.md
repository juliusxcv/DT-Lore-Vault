
## LVL 1 Autopsy:
The designated Shard Swarm originates from the native rodent species Rattus Ferratus, a resilient scavenger common to Luna Tenebris’ irradiated caverns and industrial ruins. Baseline specimens possess coarse fur interwoven with naturally metallic strands, likely an evolutionary adaptation against environmental toxins and mineral contamination. Their physiology already displayed unusual resistance to disease, radiation, and chemical exposure prior to experimentation.

## LVL 2 Autopsy:
Dissection confirms deliberate cybernetic and biochemical modification. Most specimens possess crude but functional servo-limb replacements, often replacing damaged extremities or spinal sections. Grafted directly into the dorsal tissue are miniature Mutagenic Catalyst Flasks, connected by improvised tubing that continuously feeds unstable compounds into the bloodstream.

Initial records describe the project as an attempt to produce:

Self-maintaining vermin servitors
Hazard-zone scavengers
Disposable reconnaissance organisms

The modifications proved remarkably successful.

## LVL 3 Autopsy:
Individual specimens display limited cognition consistent with baseline Rattus Ferratus. However, behavioral studies reveal a dramatic anomaly once multiple specimens gather.

Observed behaviors include:

Coordinated pack movement
Collective problem solving
Organized retrieval of machinery and biological matter
Deliberate augmentation of injured or unmodified rodents

Most alarming is their apparent ability to conduct crude surgery. Servo limbs and mutagenic grafts appear installed not by human handlers, but by the swarm itself. Unmodified Rattus Ferratus introduced into infested sectors frequently disappear, only to re-emerge altered and incorporated into the collective.

No centralized control organism has yet been identified.

## LVL 4 Autopsy:
Recovered laboratory records place the Shard Swarm among Harridan and the Magos Biologis’ early servitor programs. Their intent was economical: replacing expensive cyber-servitors with self-sustaining biological constructs capable of maintenance, scouting, and hazardous labor. Initial trials met all expectations.

Then containment reports begin.

Specimens ignored handler commands, dismantled restraint systems, and demonstrated escalating coordination. More troubling still, they began harvesting components and mutagenic reservoirs without instruction, spreading augmentation throughout surrounding Rattus Ferratus populations.
