---

### Trial Summary

Beta-Kappa stimulant compounds continue to demonstrate favorable battlefield performance during controlled exposure studies.

Primary objectives include:

- Increased pain resistance
- Suppression of fatigue response
- Enhanced aggression and combat persistence
- Temporary resilience to traumatic injury
- Delayed shock and blood-loss collapse

Human test subjects displayed measurable improvements in combat endurance and recovery potential during sustained engagement simulations.

Observed fatalities remain below projected attrition thresholds.

### Arboretum Livefire Evaluation

Following laboratory trials, stimulant-treated subjects transferred to the **Arboretum Combat Zone** for practical assessment.

The Arboretum remains configured as a controlled environmental proving ground featuring:

- Dense vegetation sectors
- Artificial marshland and ravine systems
- Variable atmospheric conditions
- Concealed firing corridors and observation stations

Livefire engagements conducted against dedicated **Shadow Sentinel** formations under Security Command supervision.

Trial objectives prioritize observation of:

- Hunting behavior
- Pack coordination
- Stimulant persistence under battlefield stress
- Combat effectiveness against trained opposition

---

### Security Liaison Report

Shadow Sentinel detachments report improved specimen lethality compared to prior trial cycles.

Multiple engagements required emergency intervention following loss of stimulant suppression controls.

Three Sentinel squads sustained severe casualties during recent Arboretum exercises.

No security breach occurred.

Replacement personnel assigned.

---

### Medicae Remarks

Common side effects continue to include:

- Organ strain
- Cognitive degradation
- Hyper-aggression
- Respiratory failure
- Post-trial neurological collapse

These outcomes remain categorized as operationally acceptable.
