### Entry — First Weeks After Completion
The buildings are finished.

Not polished—nothing ever is—but standing, sealed, and holding heat. That feels like an achievement worth recording. The wind still finds its way into places it shouldn’t, and the light outside does that strange soft thing at all hours, but inside the settlement there is structure now. Angles. Corners. Routine.

House II smells like lubricant and boiled grain most of the time. I am told this is unpleasant. I find it reassuring.

We ran the first full systems test today. Power held. Air recyclers stabilized after some adjustment. The Valev processors are louder than projected, but within tolerance. I would rather loud than quiet.

The children are already using the rocks outside as playground. I pretended to scold them, then marked the rocks in the grass that bruise shins so I can remove them later. Mila says I am incapable of not turning everything into a checklist.

She is correct.

The chapel was completed yesterday. Smaller than the habitation houses, but better built. Someone—Karsen, I think—insisted the stonework be overengineered “just in case.” No one argued. [[Saint Dravik the Lantern‑Bearer of the Deep]] watches over the room with that stubborn, patient expression the sculptor gave him. The lantern at his side is not lit yet. That will come later, before the first descent is made.

There was an argument at supper about who gets which shifts once extraction begins. Everyone wants to be first down. I understand that feeling. The ore veins are mapped and promising, and there is something deeply satisfying about finally digging into stone you’ve stared at for months.

My brother Jorren keeps talking about bonuses already. I reminded him that ore in the ground has never paid anyone. He laughed and said that was why he married into the Renns instead of the Valevs.

Children laughed too, even though they didn’t understand.

Small things:  
– The water tastes metallic but clean.  
– Some of the glowsticks arrived cracked, but most are still usable.  
– Someone painted a mural of the moon’s horizon in the mess hall. The colors are wrong, but only slightly.

Tomorrow we begin the first shallow cuts. Nothing heroic. Nothing dangerous, if the scans are to be believed. Just work. Just repetition. Just the slow turning of effort into result.

It feels good to build something that is meant to last.

I think we will be happy here, at least for a while.
