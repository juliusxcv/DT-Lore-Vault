Author: Nullis Helbrecht

Blessed is the mind too small for doubt, for mine is filled with the visions of this day’s carnage.
We walked the dark, The Doomtroopers and I. A motley collection of souls, bound by the Golden Throne’s necessity, if not by grace.

I watched the shriveling leaf of a man—that wretched 'Veteran'—stumble like a blind grox into a pool of caustic sludge. Pathetic. To lack the basic discipline of footfall is to insult the very ground the Emperor provided. My scorn was righteous, yet the mission ground on.

The hum of the hive’s blood returned as we restored the generators. I must confess, the Steel-Soul—the machine-servant—was the only one among them with a shred of purpose. It did not complain; it did not bleed; it simply obeyed. A cold piety, but piety nonetheless.
But then, the Emperor tested the clumsy one again. He stepped into a dwelling-shrine turned tomb. The walking rot—the dead who refuse to lie still—surged from the floorboards and dragged him into the filth. He fell, his screams a testament to his weakness.

I felt a chill then, my Lord. It could have been me. I had the same path intended. I nearly stepped where he stepped. But Your hand stayed my foot. Your light blinded me to that path so I might walk another. Why spare the faithful and strike the fool? I do not ask; I only serve.
Then, the Witch. The girl with the fractured mind. She unleashed the Warp-fire, lightning dancing from her fingertips like the Emperor’s own wrath made manifest. The risen dead were scorched into ash in a heartbeat. I have loathed her kind, but today... today she was a vessel of Your storm. Perhaps even a spark can be useful before it is extinguished.

Keep my feet steady, my blade sharp, and my hate pure.

For the Emperor.
